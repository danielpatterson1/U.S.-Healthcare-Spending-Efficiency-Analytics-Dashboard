# 🏥 U.S. Healthcare Costs, Payments, and Operational Efficiency  
### Interactive Analytics Dashboard | Executive README  

---

## 📌 Overview
This project presents an interactive dashboard analyzing long-term trends in U.S. healthcare spending, with a focus on:

- How healthcare costs have evolved over time  
- How spending growth compares to population and income growth  
- Who pays for healthcare services  
- Which services drive the majority of spending and spending growth  

The dashboard combines historical CMS data with interactive visualizations to support system-level understanding and decision-ready insights.

---

## 🎯 Purpose
Healthcare spending is often discussed through individual metrics in isolation. This project was designed to show how costs, payments, and growth drivers interact across the full system.

The primary objectives were to:

- Place healthcare spending in broader economic context  
- Separate spending levels from spending growth  
- Distinguish population-driven growth from per-capita cost growth  
- Show how payment responsibility varies by service  
- Translate complex CMS tables into an accessible analytical framework  

> This dashboard is exploratory and analytical in nature. It does not provide policy recommendations.

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

### Spending Continues to Rise Structurally
- U.S. healthcare spending exceeds **$5T annually**  
- Healthcare represents approximately **18% of U.S. GDP**  
- Spending trends continue upward even after inflation adjustment  

### Per-Capita Costs Drive Growth
- Most total spending growth is driven by **higher cost per person**  
- Population growth contributes significantly less  

### Costs Are Highly Concentrated
- Hospitals and physician/clinical services dominate total spending  
- A small number of categories drive most incremental growth  

### Public Programs Matter More Over Time
- Medicare and Medicaid fund roughly **one-third of total spending**  
- Payment responsibility varies widely by service type  

### Healthcare Costs Outpace Income Growth
- Spending per person has grown faster than GDP per capita  
- The affordability gap continues to widen

## 🧭 How to Read the Dashboard
The dashboard is organized for both high-level insight and deeper analysis:

- **Summary metrics** → snapshot of costs, growth, and funding mix  
- **Time-series charts** → nominal vs. inflation-adjusted trends  
- **Growth decomposition** → population vs. per-capita drivers  
- **Service-level analysis** → cost concentration  
- **Payer views** → Medicare, Medicaid, private, and out-of-pocket  

Interactive controls allow filtering by time horizon and metrics.

---

## 🧪 Data & Methodology
- **Source:** CMS National Health Expenditure Accounts (NHEA)  
- Includes total spending, per-capita spending, payer mix, GDP, and population  
- Inflation adjustments applied using historical CPI indices  
- Cross-validation performed across multiple CMS tables
